Proposal

Renaming Our Full-Tuition Award the EMU Honors Scholarship

Luke Litwiller · Office of Undergraduate Admissions · June 2026

The ask What it is Why change Peers Timeline Heritage Discuss

The ask

I’m proposing we formally rename our full-tuition award from the Yoder/Webb Scholarship to the EMU Honors Scholarship, effective the Fall 2027 award cycle, with the new name in the market by July 2026. The change ties the award directly to the relaunched Honors Program, makes it instantly clear to families what the scholarship is, and reflects that the award is now largely funded by the university.

Section 1

What the award is today

This is EMU’s highest academic award: a full-tuition scholarship for all four years, selected by our honors faculty through a portfolio, essay, video, and one-on-one interview. Recipients will already be called Honors Scholars and graduate with an Honors cord with the newly developed Honors program.

Section 2

Why change the name

1Clarity for students and families

“Yoder/Webb” tells a prospective student very little about what the award is or how it connects to their path at EMU. “Honors Scholarship” does both at a glance: it signals the academic level, links straight to the Honors Program, and matches how families actually search and how our competitors name their flagship awards.

2It reflects how the award is funded now

The scholarship is largely institutionally funded at this point. With no single endowed gift carrying the full cost, a program-based name is both more accurate and more flexible for how we fund and award it going forward.

Open question · Advancement I’d like this cross-referenced by the Advancement team to ensure we are still satisfying donor-intent / stewardship if we make this change.

3Timing with the Honors relaunch

The reimagined Honors Program rolls out Fall 2026. Renaming the award now lets the scholarship and the program share one identity from day one, instead of asking students to connect “Yoder/Webb” to “Honors” on their own.

Section 3

How peers name their top awards

I looked at comparable Mennonite and Shenandoah Valley institutions. The pattern is consistent: top full-tuition awards are named descriptively and tied to honors, never by an internal personal name families can’t decode.

InstitutionTop full-tuition awardHow it’s framed
Goshen CollegeMennonite peer President’s Leadership Award Sits atop plainly tiered academic awards: Dean’s, High Honors, Honors, and Academic Excellence.
Messiah University Trustees’ Scholarship (full tuition) Awarded through the Honors Program, alongside the President’s Scholarship for College Honors.
Bridgewater CollegeShenandoah Valley peer President’s Merit Award Open only to members of the Flory Honors Program.
Bluffton UniversityMennonite peer Presidential Scholarship Full-tuition award decided through the Presidential Scholarship Competition.
Takeaway: “Honors Scholarship” puts us squarely in line with the schools similar to us. Goshen, a fellow Mennonite college, is the clearest case - plain, program-based names that families understand on the first read.

Section 4

Heritage statement

Proposed credit line for the website and brochures, honoring the founding gift and the Webb legacy.

The EMU Honors Scholarship, rooted in academic excellence and belonging, was founded in 1992 through the generous support of EMU alumni Dr. Paul and Carol Yoder, whose endowed gift has helped sustain the program for more than three decades. The award also carries forward the legacy of the Webb Scholars: Ada Webb, one of the first African-American students to attend EMU, and Margaret ‘Peggy’ Webb, EMU’s first African-American graduate in 1954.
